What are carbon emissions and what do they do?

"Carbon Emissions" is a combined measure of carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane (CH4).
Fact: When CO2 dissolves in the ocean, it acidifies the ocean,
weakening the shells of crustaceans at the base of the food chain.
